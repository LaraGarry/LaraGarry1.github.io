      The target audiance for the cultural heritage preservation website comprimises individuals of varying ages and backgrounds, with a focus on the younger generation, middle aged adults, along with seniors. The rationale behind this choice is the widespread accesbility of smartphone photography. Younger individuals, especially contnet creators and micro-influencers, are alreadt engaging in cultural documentation through platfoorms like tiktok and instagram. The website aims to raise awareness among them about the importance of their contributions. Building audience personas reveals distinct habits, motivations, and potential challenges. for the younger demographic, the site will emphasize the inherent cultural preservation in their current activities, while for the 30-50 age group, it will highlighy opportunities to contribute during family outings. The 60+ age group, sterotypically, having more time and interest, could be motivated by reigniting old passions. in terms of design, the website should be sleek, easy to navigate, and visually pleasing. It will showcase Scotland's cultural heritage through multiple photos, including user-contributed content. The design will maintain a balance between visual appeal and a clean, concise layout, ensureing the message of cultural preservation takes center stage. Additionally, the site will feature case studies to exemplify the impact of photographing cultural sites. As seen in the mood boards, the website will take inspiration from National geographic and patagonia, and use the photography for much of the design, while adopting simplistic writng and fonts, to allow for the message and photos to attract many.
